Researchers have developed a new artificial intelligence system that can detect early signs of cancer with 95% accuracy, significantly outperforming traditional diagnostic methods.

The breakthrough AI model, developed at Stanford University, analyzes medical imaging scans with unprecedented precision. In clinical trials, it successfully identified malignant tumors that experienced radiologists had missed.

How It Works

The system uses deep learning algorithms trained on over 100,000 anonymized patient scans. Unlike previous models, it can detect subtle patterns invisible to the human eye and provides explanations for its diagnoses.

Clinical Impact

Early trials show the technology could reduce false negatives by up to 40% while cutting diagnosis times from weeks to hours. Hospitals in five states will begin piloting the system next month.
